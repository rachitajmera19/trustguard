Phase 01 · Empathize

Research & Interviews

We conducted 10 qualitative interviews with participants across India — uncovering lived experiences of cyberbullying, online scams, and digital manipulation. Each interview was transcribed via TurboScribe and analysed thematically.

Overview

Methodology

Interviews were conducted in-person or via audio recording across 7 core topic areas: personal experience, sense of online safety, perceived causes, reporting behaviour, suggested improvements, educational adequacy, and individual response strategies.

One participant submitted written responses. All data was analysed thematically to identify recurring patterns, barriers, and opportunities. Audio recordings were transcribed using TurboScribe for accuracy.

Key Findings

What we discovered

9/10
Had direct or indirect exposure to digital harm
Experiences ranged from Instagram fraud and phone scams to sustained cyberbullying and coordinated psychological manipulation lasting multiple days.
7/10
Did not formally report the incident
Barriers: not knowing where to report, belief authorities dismiss small scams, shame and self-blame, emotional exhaustion, fear of escalation.
2
Universal root causes across all interviews
Lack of digital literacy and psychological manipulation (urgency, authority impersonation) were cited universally as why victims fall for scams.
₹1L+
Drained from one family account in 30 minutes
A fake customer-support call exploited trust to extract funds instantly with no mechanism to freeze or reverse the transaction.
3
Days an elderly couple was psychologically manipulated
"Digital arrest" scam forced them to liquidate fixed deposits. Sustained pressure over 72 hours with no intervention or support system available.
0
Participants satisfied with school cyber education
Universal consensus: schools fail to provide practical, current digital safety education — leaving students and young adults unprepared.
