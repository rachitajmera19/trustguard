TrustGuard — Fraud-Event ID Generator

Our working prototype for the National Fraud Freeze concept. Victims generate a secure, trackable Fraud-Event ID instantly — bypassing manual FIR paperwork and giving law enforcement a standardised reference from the first minute.

256-bit Encrypted Generates ID in 1.5s Mobile-First Design Downloadable PDF Report
The Problem This Solves

Why TrustGuard exists

Today, a victim of financial fraud in India must navigate a fragmented, slow, and inaccessible reporting system. They must call their bank (on hold, multiple departments), file a cybercrime complaint online (complex UI), visit a police station (FIR paperwork), and keep track of multiple different reference numbers — all while in a state of shock.

TrustGuard collapses this entire process into a single 90-second interaction. The Fraud-Event ID it generates is a universal reference that works across banks, police portals, and cybercrime cells — eliminating the need to re-file the same information multiple times.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to use TrustGuard

Follow these steps to generate your Fraud-Event ID using the live prototype below. Each step takes seconds — total time from start to ID is under 2 minutes.

1
Open the Prototype
Scroll down to the Live Prototype section on this page. The TrustGuard form loads directly in the browser — no downloads, no login, no app required.
Scroll to "Try It Now"
2
Enter Your Full Legal Name
Type your full name exactly as it appears on your Aadhaar, PAN, or other official identification. This will appear on the PDF report and is used by authorities for verification.
Required field
3
Add Contact Information
Enter your email address or phone number. This is your contact override — the channel authorities and banks will use to reach you about your case. Use a number or email you actively monitor.
Required field
4
Select the Date of Incident
Pick the exact date the fraud occurred using the date picker. The system prevents future dates. If you're unsure of the exact date, use the closest known date. Accurate dates help law enforcement correlate transaction logs.
Cannot be a future date
5
Choose a Fraud Category
Select the category that best describes what happened. Options: Financial / Bank Fraud (UPI scam, fake calls), Identity Theft, Cyber / Phishing (fake links, OTP theft), Property Fraud, or Other.
Required — select one
6
Write a Brief Description (Optional)
A 2–4 sentence summary of what happened. Example: "I received a call from someone claiming to be from SBI. They asked me to share my OTP to prevent account blocking. I shared it and ₹25,000 was transferred." More detail = faster resolution.
Optional but recommended
7
Click "Generate Fraud ID"
Press the blue Generate Fraud ID button. A 1.5-second processing animation runs (simulating a secure backend API call). The button disables during processing to prevent double-submission.
1.5 second processing
8
View Your Fraud-Event ID
The success screen displays your unique Fraud-Event ID in the format FRD-2026-XXXX-XXXX. This ID is cryptographically generated and globally unique — no two IDs are ever the same.
Animated success confirmation
9
Copy the ID to Clipboard
Click the copy icon next to your ID. A "Copied!" tooltip confirms the copy. Paste this ID directly into your bank's fraud reporting form, police complaint portal, or share it via WhatsApp with the relevant authority.
One-click copy
10
Share with Your Bank
Call your bank's fraud helpline (e.g. SBI: 1800-11-2211) and quote your Fraud-Event ID as the case reference. The ID contains your incident category and timestamp — banks can use it to pull your report details instantly.
Step 1 on success screen
11
Share with Law Enforcement
Provide your Fraud-Event ID to the cybercrime cell (cybercrime.gov.in) or local police station. The ID replaces the need to re-explain the incident — officers look it up in the system and see the full documented record.
Step 2 on success screen
12
Download Your PDF Report
Click Download PDF to get a formatted PDF confirmation of your report. This serves as official documentation — keep a copy for your records, share with your bank's dispute team, and store it safely.
Simulated in prototype

The ID Explained

Anatomy of a Fraud-Event ID

Every ID generated by TrustGuard follows a structured format that encodes useful metadata — making it machine-readable for banks and police systems, not just a random reference number.

FRD
Prefix
"Fraud Report Document" — identifies the record type
2026
Year
Year of ID generation — enables annual record partitioning
X4K2
Segment A
4-char random alphanumeric — cryptographically generated
9MNP
Segment B
Second 4-char segment — over 1.6 billion unique combinations

Example: FRD–2026–X4K2–9MNP  |  Total unique combinations: 36⁴ × 36⁴ = 1,679,616 per year

Design Rationale

Why we designed it this way

Every design decision in TrustGuard traces back to a specific finding from our research or a constraint identified during the Define phase.

⚡
Speed Over Completeness
The form has only 5 fields — not 25. Research showed victims in distress abandon complex forms. A 5-field form with a 90-second completion time maximises the chance someone actually reports. The ID is generated immediately; deeper details can be added later.
🔒
256-bit Encryption Badge
The visible "256-bit Encrypted" badge addresses a critical trust barrier identified in interviews — participants said they don't report online because they don't trust government websites. Visual security signals directly tackle this friction point.
📋
Structured ID Over Free-Text
A machine-readable ID (FRD-YEAR-XXXX-XXXX) enables cross-system lookup — one ID works at any bank, police station, or cybercrime portal. Free-text references fail because no two systems use the same format. This is the core interoperability innovation.
📱
Mobile-First, Single Column
70% of fraud occurs via phone calls, meaning victims are on mobile when they need to report. The single-column, large-tap-target design works on small screens under stress. The max-width of 540px prevents accidental wide-screen form sprawl.
✂️
Copy-to-Clipboard for the ID
Interviews showed that victims who wrote down reference numbers often lost them or made errors. One-tap clipboard copy eliminates transcription errors when sharing the ID with bank helplines or pasting into complaint portals. The "Copied!" tooltip confirms success.
🔄
Animated Success → Trust Signal
The animated check icon and "Event Recorded" confirmation screen directly addresses finding #7 from our research: victims feel abandoned when they report and receive no acknowledgement. The animation and success state create emotional reassurance that something concrete happened.
📄
PDF Download for Paper Trails
Many elderly users and traditional authorities still require printed documentation. The PDF download bridges the digital-physical gap — giving victims a printable record they can physically hand to a bank manager or police officer, eliminating "we need something in writing."
🔁
"File Another Report" Button
Research revealed that many victims experience multiple incidents or want to report on behalf of family members. The reset flow allows multiple reports in one session — critical for elderly users who may be assisted by a family member filing several related incidents.
Roadmap

From prototype to deployment

TrustGuard is currently a front-end prototype. Deployment as a real system requires backend integration with NPCI, scheduled banks, and the National Cybercrime Portal. Here is the path forward.

→
Phase 1: User Testing (Now)
Test this prototype with 5 fraud victims and 3 elderly users. Measure: time to complete, error rate, emotional response on success screen.
→
Phase 2: Backend API
Build the fraud ID generation API with real persistence. Integrate with cybercrime.gov.in's reporting endpoint for automatic case forwarding.
→
Phase 3: Bank Integration
Pilot with 2–3 banks (ideally SBI and HDFC) to accept Fraud-Event IDs as valid case references in their fraud dispute systems.
→
Phase 4: Voice-First Layer
Add voice-guided form filling in Hindi, Telugu, and Tamil — making the system accessible to elderly and low-literacy users as per our persona research.
What the full system needs
Secure Backend Database — encrypted fraud records with cross-bank lookup capability
NPCI & Bank APIs — real-time freeze trigger within 2 minutes of report submission
Voice STT Pipeline — regional speech-to-text for dialect-aware form completion
Live Status Dashboard — victim-facing case tracker showing real-time progress after filing
OS-Level Shortcut — lock screen SOS button for zero-login access in emergencies
