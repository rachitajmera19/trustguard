Title

CYBERSABER
Design Thinking Project 2026

1. User / Protagonist

Aryan Mehta — 20, Engineering/Law undergraduate, urban India, high tech literacy.

“I'm careful online — but when something happens, I have no idea where to go.”

  • Navigate platforms safely.
  • Protect family from scams.
  • Know how to report cybercrime.

Awika Singh — 18-22, student, urban India, high tech literacy.

“I just deleted the post — I didn't know there was something I could report.”

  • Express freely without harassment.
  • Shop safely on social commerce.
  • Understand cyberbullying and legal options.

Reena Sharma — 48-58, homemaker, urban India, medium tech literacy.

“We filed a complaint, but the money was gone. I avoid online transactions now.”

  • Complete digital transactions safely.
  • Recognize scam red flags independently.
  • Not rely on children for every step.

2. Context & Situation

Setting: Urban India; students, young adults, and homemakers; heavy daily use of Instagram, WhatsApp, and UPI; informal social commerce with no payment safety net.

Triggers: Fake bank or authority calls, advance-payment Instagram scams, phishing links, body-shaming comments, fake profiles, and “digital arrest” manipulations targeting elderly users.

Response Gap: Most victims deleted or ignored instead of reporting; they did not know how to file a complaint; reporting felt too complex or futile; police rarely acted on small cases.

Awareness Gap: Schools teach theory, not practice; the cybercrime portal is unknown to most; elderly and rural users are highly exposed; families rely on younger members for decisions.

3. Evidence Base

VBIT Case, Telangana, 2022-23: Women engineering students were harassed via WhatsApp and Instagram through morphed photos and threats to leak explicit content. Four accused were arrested within days of the FIR.

Renu Desai, Hyderabad, 2025: The actress was targeted by four coordinated accounts using vulgar abuse and false propaganda. Cyberabad Cybercrime Police arrested two people under the IT Act and IPC.

Shanaya Khan, Hyderabad: A 14-year-old was bullied with sexualised comments on a birthday photo. The harassment continued after the post was deleted. She needed counselling and never formally reported it.

UN Report 2025: More than 30,000 children surveyed. 66% said cyberbullying is rising, 82% do not report due to fear of stigma, AI deepfakes create new threat vectors, and reputational harm spreads in seconds.

Legal Framework, India: IT Act Section 67 covers obscene content and Section 67A covers explicit material. IPC/BNS provisions include Section 499 for defamation and Section 354D for cyberstalking. BNS 2023 replaced IPC from July 2024.

4. Pain Points & Needs

  • Aryan: No escalation path; knows red flags but not next steps. Report button is buried deep in settings with no in-context guidance. No confirmation, ticket ID, or status update after reporting. Cybercrime portal and helpline awareness comes too late.
  • Awika: Does not know body-shaming qualifies as cyberbullying or is reportable. Fears reporting will escalate instead of resolve the situation. Copes through avoidance and self-censorship.
  • Reena: Lacks a simple, trusted digital safety guide for 45+ users. Received no real-time vishing alert from the bank and the scam call felt official. Filed an FIR but did not recover the money, creating residual fear of digital transactions.

5. Root Causes — 5 Whys

Core: No trusted path to protection or recovery.

  • L1 Main Branches: Reporting is buried, no status is visible, systems are siloed, and users are excluded.
  • L2 Immediate Causes: Report button is three or more menus deep, there is no ticket ID, and no shared fraud signal.
  • L3 Design Decisions: Safety is not on the roadmap, regional content is missing, and there is no onboarding for 45+ users.
  • L4 Systemic Gaps: Audits are shallow, RBI fines are too low, and FIRs are not linked to recovery.
  • L5 Root Incentives: Penalties are not applied, there is no enforcement SLA, and cyber insurance is missing.

6. How Might We Questions

  • HMW create a single API call that freezes funds across all linked banks within 60 seconds of a fraud report?
  • HMW give every user a one-tap SOS from the lock screen that works even in panic or low-literacy situations?
  • HMW make fraud recovery status live and publicly visible so victims stop calling and banks stay accountable?
  • HMW warn users about local scam threats in their dialect before they are targeted?
  • HMW make high-value transactions safer for elderly and low-literacy users through voice and traffic-light risk signals?
  • HMW give every user a legal 60-second right to retract a payment they were coerced into making?
  • HMW restructure fraud helplines like a SOC with playbooks, peer counsellors, and cross-bank case tracking?

7. Top Ideas

1. National Fraud Freeze RPCAn NPCI-mandated open API where one authenticated victim call fans out freeze instructions to every linked PSP simultaneously, with a 60-second SLA and an idempotent fraud-event ID replacing FIR paperwork below a defined threshold.
2. One-tap SOS Lock-Screen TriggerA freeze trigger from lock screen, Quick Settings, and a short-code voice line with no login needed. A Family Vault lets one or two verified contacts act for the victim through attested digital power of attorney.
3. Fraud-Event ID Replaces FIR PaperworkA digitally generated, shareable fraud-event ID issued at report time, recognized across banks, the cybercrime portal, and telcos to reduce victim burden from days to seconds.

8. Prototype / Flow Diagram

The proposed prototype is a digital Fraud-Event ID system that instantly generates a unique fraud case ID as soon as a victim reports cyber fraud through the app or portal. Instead of visiting a police station and filing paperwork manually, the victim gets a shareable digital ID within seconds.

  • TrustGuard secure reporting platform.
  • 256-bit encrypted security system.
  • Incident/Fraud reporting form.
  • Instant Fraud-Event ID generation.
  • Full legal name input field and contact override field.
  • Incident date calendar picker and fraud category dropdown.
  • Brief fraud description text area.
  • Secure digital complaint submission.
  • Trackable fraud case generation.
  • Authority and bank sharing support.
  • Manual paperwork bypass system.
  • Simple user-friendly interface.
  • Centralized fraud reporting dashboard.
