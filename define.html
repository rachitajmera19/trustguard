Phase 02 · Define

Problem Definition

From 118 How Might We questions and a thorough root cause analysis, we narrowed our design focus to a single, precise challenge — targeting the most critical unmet need for fraud victims in India.

Root Cause Analysis

Mapping the causes of digital harm

Root Cause Analysis (RCA) helped us move beyond symptoms to understand the systemic, platform-level, and individual-level drivers of digital harm. We identified 10 interconnected root causes from our research.

1
Platform Design Failures
Reporting flows buried deep in settings menus, not differentiated by severity or type of harm.
2
Identity & Trust Gaps
Anonymity shields perpetrators; verified badges mislead victims into trusting fraudulent accounts.
3
Accessibility Exclusions
Safety UX designed without elderly, low-literacy, or non-English speaking users in mind.
4
No Real-Time Intervention
Victims cannot trigger a cross-bank freeze or SIM-swap alert in the critical minutes after fraud.
5
Systemic Silence
No acknowledgement, ticket, or status update after reporting — erodes all trust in institutions.
6
Education & Literacy Deficit
Schools fail to teach practical, current digital safety — leaving users completely unprepared.
7
Psychological Manipulation
Scammers exploit urgency, authority, and emotional triggers that override rational thinking.
8
Inadequate Legal Speed
Existing laws require evidence collection and manual processes that move far too slowly for digital crime.
9
Shame & Under-Reporting
Victims blame themselves and avoid reporting — creating a silent, invisible scale of harm.
10
Fragmented Response Systems
Banks, police, and platforms do not communicate — leaving victims to navigate multiple systems alone.

Top 10 Ranked HMW Questions
1 HMW help victims trigger a real-time, cross-bank instant freeze within minutes of reporting digital fraud?
2 HMW give every victim an immediate acknowledgement, ticket number, and live status after reporting?
3 HMW surface an in-context risk warning before a high-risk UPI transaction is confirmed?
4 HMW alert a user and their bank in real time when their SIM is swapped?
5 HMW make fraud reporting a one-tap action accessible from the home screen or lock screen?
6 HMW give elderly users an age-aware UPI onboarding flow with simplified vocabulary and pacing?
7 HMW proactively warn users flagged as at-risk before scammers reach them?
8 HMW deliver voice and audio safety content to low-literacy users in their own dialect?
9 HMW automatically escalate a victim's report into the national cybercrime portal without re-filing?
10 HMW make screen-reader, ARIA, and focus-trap behaviour correct on every safety modal?
Scoring Rubric

Each HMW was scored 1–5 on three criteria. The top 20 questions entered a shortlist before the final selection.

Impact on Victims/5

How significantly would solving this HMW improve outcomes for the most vulnerable users?

Feasibility/5

Is this solvable within current technology, regulatory, and resource constraints in India?

Novelty/5

Does solving this create a meaningfully new solution — not just an incremental improvement to what exists?

Selected HMW — Our Design Challenge
How might we help a victim of digital fraud trigger an instant cross-bank freeze and see live status within minutes of reporting — including users who are elderly, regional, or non-literate?
