Explore the full project

Eight interconnected components spanning every stage of the Design Thinking process — click any card to explore the full detail.

Phase 01
Research & Interviews

Qualitative interviews with 10 participants across India — uncovering the lived realities of cyberbullying, online fraud, and digital manipulation. Includes participant profiles, key findings, and direct quotes.

10 interviews · 9/10 affected Explore
Phase 02
User Personas

Three composite personas synthesised from our primary research — representing a teenage social media user, an elderly digital user, and an urban working professional. Each persona guides a distinct design direction.

3 personas · 7 user types Explore
Phase 02
Problem Definition

From 118 How Might We questions to a single, focused design challenge — powered by root cause analysis, thematic clustering, and a three-criteria scoring rubric (impact, feasibility, novelty).

118 HMWs · 10 root causes Explore
Context
Case Studies

Two documented real-world cyberbullying cases from Hyderabad — Shanaya Khan (teen harassment) and Renu Desai (celebrity targeted abuse) — that grounded and shaped every design decision.

2 real cases · Hyderabad Explore
Phase 03
Ideation Workshop

A structured 6-3-5 Brainwriting session with 6 participants generating 108 ideas in 30 minutes — clustered and scored into 7 actionable solution concepts ready for prototyping.

108 ideas · 7 concepts Explore

Phase 04 · Live Prototype
TrustGuard — Fraud-Event ID

A fully working fraud reporting tool — fill in a 5-field form and generate a real Fraud-Event ID (like FRD-2026-X4K2-9MNP) you can share with banks and police. Includes a detailed how-to guide.
